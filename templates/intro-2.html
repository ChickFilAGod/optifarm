Farmer George raises two main types animals on his farm: hogs and cattle. Both can eventually be sold for a nice profit, but they require lots of time and money.

Profit

In the year of Farmer George, hogs can be sold at the market to make a profit of $25, while cattle can be sold to make $79.

Labor

Farmer George faces a challenging labor constraint: he has 600 man hours for raising animals. Each hog takes 3 hours of work, while each cow takes 12 hours of work.

Feed

Farmer George also faces a challenging feed constraint: he only has 2700 bushels of animal feed. Each hog requires 15 bushels of feed, while each cow requires 45 bushels of feed.
