Problem Information

Farmer Joseph, a poultry farmer, wants to provide the most cost-effective feed mix for his flock of chickens. He's faced with the decision of blending two types of grains: corn and soybeans. Corn, priced at $0.23 per kilogram, offers 75 grams of protein and 740 grams of carbohydrates per kilogram. Soybeans, costing $0.37 per kilogram, provide 400 grams of protein and 320 grams of carbohydrates per kilogram. Farmer Joseph needs precisely 20,000 grams of protein and 60,000 grams of carbohydrates in his batch of feed mix to keep his chickens healthy. Farmer Joseph has already set the labels and profit formula, can you help finish his work and determine the most cost-effective combination of corn and soybeans (rounded to the kilogram)?
